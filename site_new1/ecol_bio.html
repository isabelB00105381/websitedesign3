Ecology & biodiversity

The hedgerows in Fingal County account for 1200ha, of “linear woodland” cover. There is a drive to increase the woodland coverage in Fingal to 7650ha by 2030 (Fingal County Council, 2010). Our campus is an important amenity resource for the local communities, but also provides habitats for many species of flora and fauna. Buzzards and other predators visit the rough grassland areas to hunt. The abundant vegetation of these meadows provides the ideal habitat for rabbits. The grounds of the campus are planted with many grass species and plant species that are important to supporting invertebrates. The grounds are planted with Greater Knapweed (Centaurea Scabiosa)that support 16 varieties of butterflies. Parts of the grounds at ITB is designated Scrubland/Green Oasis - these marginal lands provide shelter and food for a variety of animals.

Bramble shrub supports:

• Blackbirds, thrushes, chaffinches,starlings, robins, foxes and mice eat the fruits.

• Cover for foxes.

• Flowers from May to September,fruits from August to October.

At ITB, our landscapers and hortoculturists created a wet grassland of sweet floating grass. The lake at the entrance to the college has a biologically diverse environment with water plants with sprawling, rooting stems that provide habitats for insects and food for birds.
